*   **Content-Security-Policy (CSP) as the primary focus:** Almost all changes are within the `Content-Security-Policy` header, demonstrating active management of external resources and security boundaries.
*   **Third-party Service Integration:** A consistent pattern of adding new domains to CSP directives, particularly `connect-src`, for services like privacy management, analytics, video streaming, advertising, CDNs, and static asset hosting.
*   **Environment-Specific Security Postures:**
    *   `pre` and `prod` environments generally maintain strict security with `Strict-Transport-Security`, `X-Frame-Options: DENY`, `Cross-Origin-Opener-Policy: same-origin`, and a preference for `https:` protocol.
    *   `dev` environments begin with highly permissive settings, but `smarttv.dev.js` shows a later shift towards stricter `https:` enforcement.
*   **HBBTV's Dual-Protocol Requirement:** Unlike `smarttv` environments which primarily enforce HTTPS, `hbbtv` environments (pre/prod) show a clear and deliberate pattern of explicitly allowing both `http://` and `https://` for various CSP directives, likely due to specific platform constraints or legacy content needs.
*   **Evolution of Allowed Domains:** There's a progression from explicitly listing individual subdomains to using wildcards (e.g., `*.youborafds01.com`, `*.3cat.cat`, `*.ccma.cat`) for greater flexibility and maintainability.
*   **Code Readability Improvements:** The `connect-src` directive, which grew considerably in length, was refactored in some files to use multi-line string concatenation for improved readability.
*   **Consistent Standard Headers:** Other security headers like `X-Frame-Options`, `X-Content-Type-Options`, `Referrer-Policy`, `Permissions-Policy`, `Cross-Origin-Opener-Policy`, `Cross-Origin-Resource-Policy`, and `X-XSS-Protection` remain largely constant across all configurations, with minor variations for `dev` environments to be more permissive.
